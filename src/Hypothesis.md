Hypothesis 1 — Smoking increases CHD risk
Patients who smoke regularly, especially those with a high number of cigarettes per day, are more likely to develop coronary heart disease.

Hypothesis 2 — Diabetes increases cardiovascular risk
Patients with diabetes are expected to have a higher probability of developing coronary heart disease due to metabolic complications.

Hypothesis 3 — High cholesterol contributes to CHD
Higher total cholesterol levels should be associated with a greater cardiovascular risk.

Hypothesis 4 — Men may present higher CHD risk
Male patients may show a higher CHD prevalence compared to female patients.

Hypothesis 5 — BMI may have moderate influence
Obesity and high BMI may increase CHD risk, but probably less than hypertension or smoking.

Hypothesis 6 — Age is one of the strongest predictors
Older patients are expected to have a much higher probability of developing CHD compared to younger patients.
